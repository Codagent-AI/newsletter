## Recommended Links

**[How I Use AI to Code](https://www.chrismdp.com/coding-with-ai/)** — Chris Parsons makes the best version of the "outer harness" argument: the senior engineer's job is shifting from reviewing every AI-generated diff to training the system that produces the next hundred diffs. The useful frame is not "better prompts," but durable harness assets: `AGENTS.md`/`CLAUDE.md`, skill files, tests, verification loops, and reset discipline.

The sharpest bit is his pushback on over-specified AI-era waterfall: specify the problem, constraints, users, system shape, and success criteria, not a brittle implementation script. That is exactly the line a good outer harness has to walk: enough structure to preserve intent, enough freedom for the agent to discover the solution.
